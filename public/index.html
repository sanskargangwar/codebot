Artificial Intelligence (AI) chatbots have garnered significant attention in recent years due to their ability to automate communication processes and provide scalable solutions for customer interaction, data handling, and personalized service delivery. A vast body of literature has emerged focusing on various aspects of chatbot technology, including natural language processing (NLP), machine learning, human-computer interaction, and the ethical considerations surrounding their use.

This literature review synthesizes research findings in these key areas, offering an overview of the current state of AI chatbot development, applications, and challenges.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) chatbots have emerged as powerful tools that simulate human conversation through text or voice interactions. These systems leverage advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) algorithms, machine learning, and deep learning techniques to understand, interpret, and respond to user queries in a contextually appropriate manner. AI chatbots are widely utilized across various industries, including customer service, healthcare, finance, and e-commerce, where they automate routine tasks, enhance user engagement, and provide 24/7 support.

The effectiveness of AI chatbots lies in their ability to learn from interactions, adapt to diverse communication styles, and continuously improve over time. Modern AI chatbots are capable of handling complex tasks such as booking appointments, providing product recommendations, and assisting with troubleshooting, all while maintaining conversational coherence. Despite their advancements, challenges remain in terms of achieving perfect understanding, addressing nuanced or ambiguous queries, and mitigating biases in training data.
